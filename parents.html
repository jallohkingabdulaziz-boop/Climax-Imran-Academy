Community Teachers Association (CTA) – Parents’ Guide

The Community Teachers Association is a partnership between the school and the community, especially parents, to support quality education, discipline, and the welfare of all pupils. This guide outlines how parents can actively contribute to the success of our school.

1️⃣ Purpose of the CTA

  • Strengthen the relationship between parents, teachers, and the school administration.
  • Support academic programs, school development projects, and student welfare.
  • Provide a platform for parents to share ideas, feedback, and concerns.
  • Promote discipline, respect, and community values among pupils.

2️⃣ Roles & Responsibilities of Parents in the CTA

  1. Regular Attendance at Meetings: Attend scheduled CTA meetings to stay informed and participate in decision-making.
  2. Support Learning at Home: Monitor homework, encourage reading, and provide a quiet study environment.
  3. Financial & Material Support: Contribute to agreed development funds, volunteer for projects, or donate resources.
  4. Promote Good Behaviour: Teach children respect, punctuality, and responsibility.
  5. Community Engagement: Represent the school positively in the community and encourage enrolment.

3️⃣ Parental Conduct Expectations

  • Respect teachers and school leaders at all times.
  • Address concerns through proper channels (CTA leadership or school administration).
  • Avoid spreading false information or creating conflict within the school community.
  • Support school rules and disciplinary measures.

4️⃣ Benefits of Active CTA Participation

  • Better communication between school and home.
  • Improved learning environment for children.
  • Stronger discipline and moral upbringing.
  • Access to updates on school events, policies, and opportunities.

5️⃣ CTA Meeting Procedures

  • Frequency: At least once per term.
  • Structure: Opening remarks, school updates, discussion of issues, agreement on action points.
  • Decision-Making: By consensus or majority vote where needed.

CTA Communication Channels

1️⃣ Formal Communication Channels

  • A. CTA Meetings: Once every term or as needed. Parents, teachers, school leadership, and CTA executives attend.
  • B. Official Letters & Notices: Distributed through pupils or directly to parents (invitations, fee notices, announcements).
  • C. Phone Contact:
    CTA Chairperson Contact: ……………
    School Office Contact: ……………
  • D. Parent–Teacher Conferences: Twice a year to discuss pupil progress and solutions.

2️⃣ Digital Communication Channels

  • A. WhatsApp Group (Moderated): Quick updates, reminders, and emergency alerts (school-related only).
  • B. Email Updates: For parents with email access – newsletters and notices.
  • C. School Website / Portal: Updates on events, assignments, results, and policies.

3️⃣ Community Outreach Channels

  • Announcements at churches and mosques for wider community awareness.
  • Local noticeboards in Rokel for parents without mobile access.

Rule for All Communication: Messages must be clear, respectful, and timely. Urgent matters should go directly to the CTA Chairperson or School Administration for quick resolution.

Remember: When parents and teachers work together, children achieve more academically, socially, and emotionally.
